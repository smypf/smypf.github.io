~/anxieties.html

I had an idea for something that I wanted to put together. Nothing special or complex and I it laid out in my head.

I'd been mucking around with how data storage would be done and after a little bit I had it in a place that I was happy.

But then I needed to put a frontend together and that's where the wheels came off.

First I thought I'd do it with as little Javascript as possible. Then I thought that would be a pain and decided to start learning Svelte as an alternative frontend library. I started looking into how to do testing and found that was a bit all over the place and I'd be missing out on improving my knowledge there.

Then that seemed too hard and instead I thought I'd start with a fresh installation of React as it's a library I'm more familiar with.

By that time I was overwhelmed and anxious and had nothing to show for the hour or so of umm'ing and ahh'ing I'd gone through. I'd wasted the short amount of time I had to do something worrying about things that in the long run didn't really matter

This upset me greatly. I've decided that to get over it I'm committing to producing something new for the next 30 days.

The rules:

  1. Each day something new. It can be completely from scratch or an enhancement to something existing.
  2. Put it online. It if's not something that someone else can witness it doesn't count.
  3. It doesn't matter what it is. An article, a toy application, just messing around with the design of this site or anything else I feel like.
  4. No meta-bullshit. Changes to the formatting, messing around with configuration or anything else that an end user would care about doesn't count.
  5. It doesn't have to be good. The exercise of putting something together is the goal.
  6. Incomplete work is better than no work at all. As long as it's online it's good enough
  7. Everything that is done gets listed somewhere on this site. Right now there's space on the home page so that's where it lives right now.
  8. Focus on getting one part right. Improve the next part after that.
